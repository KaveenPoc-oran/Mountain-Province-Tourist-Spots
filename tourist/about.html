About Mountain Province

Welcome to the Heart of the Cordilleras

Mountain Province is a landlocked province in the Cordillera Administrative Region, Philippines. It is known for its stunning rice terraces, limestone caves, hanging coffins, and vibrant indigenous culture. The province is home to the Bontoc, Kankana-ey, and other ethnolinguistic groups who have preserved their traditions for centuries.

Quick Facts
  • Capital: Bontoc
  • Population: ~158,000 (2020)
  • Language: Ilocano, Kankana-ey, Tagalog, English
  • Known for: Sagada Hanging Coffins, Sumaguing Cave, Maligcong Rice Terraces
Our Mission

The Mountain Province Tourist Spot Information System aims to provide accurate, up-to-date, and accessible information to all visitors. We strive to promote sustainable tourism while respecting and preserving our cultural heritage.
