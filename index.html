Sagada

Hanging Coffins
Sagada Hanging Coffins

Location: Sagada

Information:

Cultural and Spiritual Significance

Proximity to Ancestors: The primary belief is that placing the deceased high on cliff faces brings their spirits closer to their ancestors and the heavens.

Protection: Practically, hanging the coffins protected the bodies from scavenging wild animals, flooding, and historical enemies like headhunters.

Returning to the Womb: Bodies are often placed in a fetal position within small, hollowed-out pine logs.

The Ritual Process

Preparation: Elders often carve their own coffins from hollowed logs before they pass away.

The Wake: The body is placed on a wooden "death chair" for several days.

Burial Criteria: Typically reserved for elders who died of natural causes and have children and grandchildren.

Visiting the Site

The most accessible site for tourists is in Echo Valley, a short hike from the town center.

Registration: Visitors must register at the Sagada Tourist Information Center.

Guide Requirement: A local guide is mandatory for the Echo Valley Tour.

Sumaguing Cave
Sumaguing Cave

Location: Sagada

Indigenous & Cultural History

Ancestral Shelter & Rituals: The cave served the Igorot people as a place for shelter, rituals, and ceremonies.

Spiritual Significance: Considered a sacred space where the spirits of ancestors reside.

Essential Tourist Information

Operating Hours: Daily from 6:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

Best Time to Visit: Dry season (November to May).

Mandatory Requirements: Registration and local guide required.

The Spelunking Experience

The Descent: A steep, slippery walk down natural rock steps.

Rock Formations: Highlights include the "King's Curtain" and "Rice Terraces".

Echo Valley
Echo Valley

Location: Sagada

Tour Information

Standard Echo Valley Tour: Short walk to the Hanging Coffins via the Anglican Cemetery.

Fee: ₱300 for a group of up to 10 people.

Duration: 30 minutes to 1 hour.

Eco-Tour: Includes Hanging Coffins, Underground River, and coffee farms.

Visitor Essentials

Access: Entrance located behind the Episcopal Church of St. Mary the Virgin.

Safety: The trail can be slippery; sturdy footwear recommended.

Respect: This is a sacred burial ground.

Bomod-ok Falls
Bomod-ok Falls

Location: Sagada, Aguid-Pide-Fidelisan

Information: Bomod-ok Falls, also known as the "Big Falls," is Sagada's most spectacular waterfall, standing approximately 200 feet tall.

The Trek & Experience

The Trail: Hike starts in Barangay Bangaan or Fidelisan through Fidelisan Rice Terraces.

Difficulty: Moderate to challenging.

The Reward: Massive, ice-cold pool at the base where you can swim.

Fees and Logistics

Registration: Present your ₱100 Sagada Environmental Fee receipt.

Guide Fee: ₱500 for a group of up to 5 people.

Village Fee: ₱25–₱30 barangay/cultural fee.

Pongas Falls
Pongas Falls

Location: Sagada

The Trek & Experience

The Path: Trek begins at the Ankileng jump-off point through traditional villages and rice terraces.

Difficulty: Moderate to challenging.

The Falls: Features multiple tiers with natural pools perfect for swimming.

Marlboro Hills (Kamanbaneng Peak)
Marlboro Hills (Kamanbaneng Peak)

Location: Sagada

History & Culture

Ancestral Grazing Land: Historically served as communal grazing land for cows and horses.

Name Origin: Popularized by early tourists comparing it to Marlboro cigarette commercials.

The Experience

The Sunrise: Most hikers start at 4:00 AM to reach the summit before dawn.

The Blue Soil: Distinct bluish-green tint caused by high copper sulfate levels.

Logistics & Fees

Registration: Present your ₱100 Environmental Fee receipt.

Guide Fees: ₱800 for up to 3 people for sunrise tour.

Transportation: Two-way shuttle approximately ₱650.

Kiltepan Viewpoint
Kiltepan Viewpoint

Location: Sagada

Cultural History

Name Origin: Portmanteau of three surrounding barangays: Kilong, Tep-an, and Antadao.

Pop Culture: Featured in the 2014 Filipino film "That Thing Called Tadhana".

Visiting Kiltepan

Best Time: Arrive by 4:00 AM for sunrise (5:30 AM - 6:00 AM).

The View: Witness a vast ocean of white clouds covering the rice terraces.

Accessibility: Approximately 3 km from the town center.

Tour Rates & Fees

Environmental Fee: ₱100 at the Sagada Tourist Information Center.

Shuttle: Two-way service costs around ₱650 per van.

Lake Danum
Lake Danum

Location: Sagada

History and Cultural Significance

Geological Origin: Natural retention lake formed by rainwater accumulation.

Ancestral Water Source: Vital water source for grazing animals historically.

Spiritual Connection: Respected as a dwelling place for nature spirits.

Information for Visitors

The Name: "Danum" translates to "water" in Kankanaey.

Sunset Viewing: Famous for its late afternoon glow.

Blue Soil Hills
Blue Soil Hills

Location: Sagada

History & Geology

Chemical Composition: Blue-green color from high concentrations of copper sulfate.

Geological Timeline: Part of ancient seabed that rose over millions of years.

Essential Visitor Information

Access: Usually visited via Marlboro Hills to Blue Soil Traverse (4–5 hours).

Tour Fees: ₱1,600 for a group of up to 3 people for traverse.

Strict Rules for Preservation

No Walking on Slopes: Stay on designated brown-soil paths.

No Collecting: Taking soil is punishable offense.

Littering: "Leave No Trace" principles strictly enforced.

Balangagan Cave

Location: Sagada

History and Lore

The Legend: Named after three tribal elders who had a spiritual encounter in the cave.

Bontoc

Bontoc Museum
Bontoc Museum

Location: Bontoc

History of the Museum

Founded in 1975 by Sister Catherine Lambrecht to preserve indigenous heritage.

The museum features black-and-white photographs showing life in the Cordilleras.

Key Exhibits

Warrior Artifacts: Traditional headhunting axes, shields, and spears.

Textiles and Weaving: Displays of traditional garments and looms.

Outdoor Heritage Village: Authentic reconstructions of traditional houses.

Practical Tips

Location: Easily accessible by public jeepney or bus from Sagada.

No Photography Indoors: Strictly prohibited to protect artifacts.

Maligcong Rice Terraces
Maligcong Rice Terraces

Location: Bontoc

History and Cultural Significance

Stone-walled terraces using unique dry-stone walling technique without mortar.

Hand-carved by the Bontoc people generations ago.

Still practices traditional organic rice farming.

Information for Visitors

The View: Best viewpoint is from the summit of Mt. Kupapey.

Guide Fee: ₱500 for a group (up to 5 people) for the Mt. Kupapey trek.

Registration Fee: ₱50 per person at local tourism office.

Mount Kupapey
Mount Kupapey

Location: Bontoc

History & Cultural Context

Spiritual Name: "Kupapey" refers to a type of butterfly abundant on the mountain.

Ancestral Hunting Grounds: Served as communal hunting grounds historically.

Key Information

The Experience: 360-degree view of Maligcong Rice Terraces and Chico River.

The Trek: Easy to Moderate, 1 to 1.5 hours to summit.

Starting Point: Most hikers start at 4:30 AM for sunrise.

Alab Petroglyphs
Alab Petroglyphs

Location: Bontoc

Information: The Alab Petroglyphs are ancient rock carvings believed to be over a thousand years old, depicting human and animal figures. They are a National Cultural Treasure of the Philippines.

Historical Significance: These petroglyphs provide insight into the ancient cultures of the Cordillera region.

Access: Requires a guided trek from Bontoc town proper.

Mount Amuyao

Fowa-as Falls
Fowa-as Falls

Location: Barlig

Information: Fowa-as Falls is a hidden gem, accessible via a trek through lush forests and rice terraces. The falls are a source of irrigation and are considered sacred by the local community.

Barlig Rice Terraces
Barlig Rice Terraces

Location: Barlig

History & Cultural Significance

Ancient Engineering: Mix of stone and earth-walled structures.

The "Ladder" Concept: Extremely vertical and narrow terraces.

Preservation through Isolation: Avoided commercialization due to remote location.

Key Sites & Information

Lias and Kadaclan Terraces: Most extensive in the area.

Logistics: Mandatory registration at Barlig Municipal Hall.

Guide Fee: Typically ₱500–₱800 depending on village trek.

Bauko

Mount Kalawitan
Mount Kalawitan

Location: Bauko

History & Cultural Significance

The "To Climb" Origin: Name derived from Kankanaey word "kalawit" meaning "to climb".

Training Ground: Used by elite mountaineers including First Philippine Everest Expedition Team.

The Hiking Experience

Trail Options: Sabangan Trail (most popular) and Talubin/Bayyo Trail.

Difficulty: Rated as Major Climb (7/9 difficulty).

Terrain: Pine forests, mossy forest, and dwarf bamboo summit.

Mount Polis
Mount Polis

Location: Bauko

Historical Significance

Strategic World War II Site: Critical defensive position during Battle of the Cordilleras.

Ancient Gateway: Primary transit point for indigenous groups for centuries.

Visitor Information

The Viewpoint: Stopover point on bus route between Banaue and Bontoc.

Climate: One of coldest spots on the road, frequently foggy.

The Hike: Easy to Moderate, 1–2 hours to summit.

Mount Data National Park
Mount Data National Park

Location: Bauko

History & Cultural Significance

Establishment: Originally declared in 1936 as "Baguio-Bontoc Scenic Park".

Peace Accord: Site of 1986 Mount Data Peace Accord between government and Cordillera People's Liberation Army.

Key Visitor Information

Mt. Data Hotel: Historic lodge serving as main gateway and visitor center.

Attractions: Mossy & Pine Forests, Inudey & Ampasit Falls, hiking trails.

Conservation Status: Strict monitoring to restore biodiversity.

Tadian

Tadian Rice Terraces
Tadian Rice Terraces

Location: Tadian

History and Cultural Significance

Legend of "Holy Water": Famous for Cagubatan Holy Water spring that never runs dry.

Stone-Walling: Built using dry-stone masonry technique.

Ancient Origin: Already mature when Spanish arrived in 1668.

Key Sites & Information

Dacudac Rice Terraces: Called "Amphitheater of Tadian".

Cagubatan Terraces: Irrigated by "Holy Water" spring.

Access: 1.5 to 2-hour drive from Sagada or Bontoc.

Mt. Mogao
